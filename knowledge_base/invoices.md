# Invoices

## What is an Invoice?
An invoice is a commercial document issued by a seller to a buyer, indicating the products, quantities, and agreed prices for products or services the seller has provided. It serves as a request for payment and a legal record of a transaction.

## Types of Invoices

### Standard Invoice
The most common type, used for one-time sales of goods or services. Includes itemized list of products/services, quantities, prices, and payment terms.

### Proforma Invoice
A preliminary invoice sent before delivery of goods or services. It outlines the expected cost and is often used for customs purposes in international trade.

### Recurring Invoice
Used for ongoing services or subscriptions. Automatically generated at regular intervals (weekly, monthly, annually).

### Credit Invoice (Credit Note)
Issued to reduce the amount a customer owes. Used when goods are returned, services were unsatisfactory, or an error was made on a previous invoice.

### Debit Invoice (Debit Note)
Increases the amount owed by a customer. Used when the original invoice was undercharged.

## Essential Elements of an Invoice
1. **Invoice Number**: A unique identifier for tracking
2. **Invoice Date**: When the invoice was issued
3. **Due Date**: When payment is expected
4. **Seller Information**: Name, address, contact details, tax ID
5. **Buyer Information**: Name, address, contact details
6. **Line Items**: Description, quantity, unit price for each product/service
7. **Subtotal**: Sum before taxes and discounts
8. **Taxes**: GST, VAT, or other applicable taxes
9. **Discounts**: Any reductions applied
10. **Total Amount Due**: Final amount the buyer must pay
11. **Payment Terms**: Accepted payment methods and conditions
12. **Notes**: Any additional information or terms

## Payment Terms Explained
- **Net 30**: Payment due within 30 days of invoice date
- **Net 60**: Payment due within 60 days
- **Due on Receipt**: Payment expected immediately
- **2/10 Net 30**: 2% discount if paid within 10 days, otherwise full amount due in 30 days

## Invoice Tracking and Management
- Use invoice numbers sequentially for easy tracking
- Follow up on overdue invoices promptly
- Keep copies of all invoices for tax purposes
- Reconcile invoices with bank statements regularly
- Record invoices in accounts receivable when issued, mark as paid when payment received

## GST/VAT on Invoices
Tax invoices must include the seller's GST/VAT registration number and clearly show the tax amount. Buyers can use these to claim input tax credits.
