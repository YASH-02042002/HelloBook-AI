# Taxation & GST in Accounting

## Business Taxes Overview
Businesses are subject to various taxes depending on their structure, location, and activities. Understanding tax obligations is essential for compliance and financial planning.

## Types of Business Taxes

### Income Tax
Tax on the profits earned by a business.
- **Sole Proprietors/Partnerships**: Business income is taxed as personal income
- **Companies/Corporations**: Subject to corporate tax rates
- Tax is calculated on net profit (revenue minus allowable deductions)

### Goods and Services Tax (GST)
A value-added tax levied on most goods and services at each stage of production/distribution.

#### How GST Works
- Businesses collect GST from customers (Output GST)
- Businesses pay GST to suppliers (Input GST)
- **Net GST Payable = Output GST - Input GST**
- If Input GST > Output GST, the business gets a refund

#### GST Registration
- Mandatory once annual turnover exceeds the threshold (varies by country)
- Voluntary registration available for smaller businesses
- Registered businesses must file regular GST returns

### Payroll Tax (TDS/PAYE)
Tax withheld from employee wages:
- Employer deducts tax at source
- Remitted to the tax authority on behalf of employees
- Includes contributions to social security, provident fund, etc.

### Withholding Tax
Tax deducted at source on certain payments:
- Payments to contractors and freelancers
- Dividend and interest payments
- Cross-border payments

## Tax Deductible Business Expenses
Common expenses that reduce taxable income:
- Office rent and utilities
- Employee salaries
- Business travel
- Marketing and advertising
- Professional fees (accountants, lawyers)
- Depreciation on business assets
- Insurance premiums
- Research and development costs

## Depreciation in Taxation

### Straight-Line Method
**(Cost - Salvage Value) / Useful Life**
Equal depreciation each year.

### Declining Balance Method
A fixed percentage applied to the book value each year. Results in higher depreciation early in the asset's life.

## Tax Planning vs Tax Evasion
- **Tax Planning (Legal)**: Using available deductions, credits, and strategies to minimize tax legally
- **Tax Avoidance (Gray area)**: Exploiting loopholes
- **Tax Evasion (Illegal)**: Concealing income or falsifying records

## Record Keeping for Tax Purposes
- Maintain all receipts and invoices for at least 5–7 years
- Keep records of all income and expenses
- Retain bank statements, payroll records, and asset registers
- Document business use of personal assets (vehicles, phones)

## Tax Deadlines and Compliance
- File tax returns by the due date to avoid penalties
- Make advance tax payments if required
- Lodge GST returns (monthly, quarterly, or annually)
- Issue TDS certificates to employees and contractors

## Common Tax Mistakes
1. Missing deadlines (incurs interest and penalties)
2. Not keeping adequate records
3. Claiming personal expenses as business deductions
4. Incorrect GST classification of goods/services
5. Not reconciling tax accounts with the general ledger
