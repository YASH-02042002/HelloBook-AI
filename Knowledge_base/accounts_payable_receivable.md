# Accounts Payable & Accounts Receivable

## Accounts Receivable (AR)

### What is Accounts Receivable?
Accounts Receivable represents money owed to a business by its customers for goods or services delivered but not yet paid for. It is classified as a current asset on the balance sheet.

### AR Process
1. Deliver goods/services to customer
2. Issue invoice with payment terms
3. Record the invoice in AR ledger
4. Send payment reminders as due date approaches
5. Record payment when received
6. Reconcile AR regularly

### Key AR Metrics
- **Days Sales Outstanding (DSO)**: Average number of days to collect payment
  - **DSO = (AR / Net Credit Sales) × Number of Days**
  - Lower DSO = faster collection
- **AR Turnover Ratio**: How often AR is collected per period
  - **AR Turnover = Net Credit Sales / Average AR**

### AR Aging Report
Groups receivables by how long they've been outstanding:
- 0–30 days (current)
- 31–60 days (slightly overdue)
- 61–90 days (overdue)
- 90+ days (seriously overdue — possible bad debt)

### Bad Debts
When a customer fails to pay, the AR must be written off as a bad debt expense. Businesses may set up an **Allowance for Doubtful Accounts** to estimate expected bad debts.

## Accounts Payable (AP)

### What is Accounts Payable?
Accounts Payable represents money a business owes to its suppliers or vendors for goods/services received but not yet paid for. It is a current liability on the balance sheet.

### AP Process
1. Receive goods/services from supplier
2. Receive supplier invoice
3. Verify invoice against purchase order (3-way matching)
4. Record the liability in AP ledger
5. Approve invoice for payment
6. Process payment by due date
7. Reconcile AP with supplier statements

### Key AP Metrics
- **Days Payable Outstanding (DPO)**: Average number of days to pay suppliers
  - **DPO = (AP / COGS) × Number of Days**
  - Higher DPO = business retains cash longer
- **AP Turnover Ratio**: How often AP is paid per period
  - **AP Turnover = Total Purchases / Average AP**

### AP Best Practices
- Pay on time to maintain good supplier relationships
- Take advantage of early payment discounts
- Avoid duplicate payments
- Maintain proper documentation for all purchases
- Reconcile statements with suppliers monthly

## AR vs AP Comparison

| Aspect | Accounts Receivable | Accounts Payable |
|--------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Nature | Asset (money owed TO you) | Liability (money YOU owe) |
| Balance Sheet | Current Asset | Current Liability |
| Represents | Customer debts | Supplier debts |
| Goal | Collect quickly | Pay strategically |
| Impact on Cash | Positive when collected | Negative when paid |

## Working Capital Impact
Effective AR and AP management is crucial for working capital:
- Collect AR faster → more cash available
- Extend AP terms → retain cash longer
- Balance both to optimize cash flow
