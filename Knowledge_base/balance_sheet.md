# Balance Sheet

## What is a Balance Sheet?
A balance sheet is a financial statement that shows a company's assets, liabilities, and shareholders' equity at a specific point in time. It provides a snapshot of what the company owns and owes, as well as the amount invested by shareholders.

## The Fundamental Accounting Equation
**Assets = Liabilities + Shareholders' Equity**
This equation must always balance — hence the name "balance sheet."

## Components of a Balance Sheet

### Assets
Everything the company owns that has economic value.

#### Current Assets (Short-term, convertible to cash within 1 year)
- **Cash and Cash Equivalents**: Money in hand and bank accounts
- **Accounts Receivable**: Money owed by customers
- **Inventory**: Goods available for sale
- **Prepaid Expenses**: Expenses paid in advance (e.g., insurance)
- **Short-term Investments**: Securities maturing within a year

#### Non-Current Assets (Long-term assets)
- **Property, Plant & Equipment (PP&E)**: Buildings, machinery, vehicles
- **Intangible Assets**: Patents, trademarks, goodwill
- **Long-term Investments**: Investments in other companies
- **Deferred Tax Assets**: Future tax benefits

### Liabilities
Everything the company owes to external parties.

#### Current Liabilities (Due within 1 year)
- **Accounts Payable**: Money owed to suppliers
- **Short-term Loans**: Borrowings due within a year
- **Accrued Expenses**: Expenses incurred but not yet paid (wages, utilities)
- **Deferred Revenue**: Payment received for services not yet delivered
- **Current Portion of Long-term Debt**: Portion of long-term debt due this year

#### Non-Current Liabilities (Due after 1 year)
- **Long-term Debt**: Mortgages, bonds payable
- **Deferred Tax Liabilities**: Future tax obligations
- **Pension Obligations**: Long-term employee benefit liabilities

### Shareholders' Equity
The residual interest in the assets after deducting liabilities.
- **Share Capital**: Amount invested by shareholders
- **Retained Earnings**: Accumulated profits not distributed as dividends
- **Additional Paid-in Capital**: Amount received above par value of shares
- **Treasury Stock**: Company's own shares repurchased (shown as negative)

## Key Balance Sheet Ratios

### Current Ratio
**Current Assets / Current Liabilities**
A ratio above 1 means the company can cover short-term obligations.

### Debt-to-Equity Ratio
**Total Liabilities / Shareholders' Equity**
Measures financial leverage; higher ratio = more debt-dependent.

### Working Capital
**Current Assets - Current Liabilities**
Positive working capital means short-term financial health.

### Return on Equity (ROE)
**Net Income / Shareholders' Equity**
Measures profitability relative to equity.

## Balance Sheet vs P&L
- **Balance Sheet**: Snapshot at a specific date (what you own and owe)
- **P&L Statement**: Performance over a period (income and expenses)
Both are essential for complete financial analysis.
