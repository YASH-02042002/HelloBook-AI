# Financial Ratios & Analysis

## What are Financial Ratios?
Financial ratios are mathematical comparisons of financial statement accounts or categories. They help evaluate a company's performance, liquidity, profitability, and solvency.

## Categories of Financial Ratios

---

## 1. Liquidity Ratios
Measure ability to meet short-term obligations.

### Current Ratio
**Current Assets / Current Liabilities**
- Above 1: Can cover short-term debts
- Ideal range: 1.5 to 2.0

### Quick Ratio (Acid-Test Ratio)
**(Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities**
- More conservative than current ratio (excludes inventory)
- Ideal: Above 1.0

### Cash Ratio
**Cash and Equivalents / Current Liabilities**
- Most conservative liquidity measure
- Shows ability to pay debts with cash alone

---

## 2. Profitability Ratios
Measure ability to generate profit.

### Gross Profit Margin
**(Gross Profit / Revenue) × 100**
- Shows production efficiency
- Higher = better control of production costs

### Net Profit Margin
**(Net Profit / Revenue) × 100**
- Overall profitability after all expenses
- Higher = more profitable

### Return on Assets (ROA)
**Net Income / Total Assets**
- How efficiently assets generate profit

### Return on Equity (ROE)
**Net Income / Shareholders' Equity**
- Returns generated on shareholders' investment
- Commonly benchmarked at 15%+

### EBITDA Margin
**EBITDA / Revenue**
- Operational profitability excluding non-cash items

---

## 3. Solvency / Leverage Ratios
Measure long-term financial stability and debt levels.

### Debt-to-Equity Ratio
**Total Debt / Shareholders' Equity**
- Higher ratio = more leveraged (higher risk)
- Varies by industry; manufacturing often has higher ratios

### Debt-to-Assets Ratio
**Total Liabilities / Total Assets**
- Proportion of assets financed by debt
- Below 0.5 generally considered safe

### Interest Coverage Ratio
**EBIT / Interest Expense**
- How many times earnings cover interest payments
- Above 3 is generally healthy

---

## 4. Efficiency Ratios (Activity Ratios)
Measure how effectively the company uses its assets.

### Inventory Turnover
**COGS / Average Inventory**
- How many times inventory is sold per period
- Higher = faster-moving inventory

### Receivables Turnover
**Net Credit Sales / Average Accounts Receivable**
- How often AR is collected per period
- Higher = faster collections

### Asset Turnover
**Revenue / Total Assets**
- Revenue generated per dollar of assets
- Higher = more efficient use of assets

---

## 5. Market / Valuation Ratios
Used for publicly listed companies.

### Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio
**Share Price / Earnings Per Share (EPS)**
- How much investors pay per dollar of earnings
- Higher P/E = growth expectations are high

### Earnings Per Share (EPS)
**Net Income / Weighted Average Shares Outstanding**
- Profit allocated to each share

### Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio
**Share Price / Book Value Per Share**
- Compares market value to book value

---

## Using Ratios Effectively
- **Compare over time**: Spot trends within the same business
- **Benchmark against industry**: Compare with similar businesses
- **Use multiple ratios**: No single ratio tells the full story
- **Consider context**: Economic conditions, business lifecycle stage
- **Combine with qualitative analysis**: Management quality, market position

## Limitations of Financial Ratios
- Based on historical data (backward-looking)
- Can be manipulated through accounting choices
- Industry comparisons can be misleading
- Don't capture qualitative factors (management, brand)
- Inflation can distort comparisons over time
